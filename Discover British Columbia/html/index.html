Historical information

Indigenous peoples, who lived and developed in British Columbia for thousands of years ,are the first to be mentioned in the province's history. Before becoming a part of Canada in 1871, British Columbia was a British colony.

In the 1750s, adventurers from the USA, Britain, Spain, and Russia started traveling to the region.The British founded Vancouver Island in 1849. The Canadian Pacific Railway opened the nation from the west to the east in 1885. The 20th century was a period of development. Therefore, it leads to a lot of people moved to British Columbia in order to take advantage of quality of life and economic opportunity.In addition, there is sad news that there is no national anthem of British Columbia, but Canada's anthem includes all of Canada's provinces.

Queen Elizabeth park

Queen Elizabeth park must see for visitors who want to visit Vancouver. Therefore, traveler is also enjoy landscape, animals, and fall season in the park. If tourists arrive around the fall, when the weather is becoming a little chilly and the trees' colors are changing, they may capture a picture of the beautiful surroundings.Therefore, this is a place where attracted many visitors come from another country come there and enjoy it. Yoho National Park - Emerald Lake

One of Canada's most stunning lakes, Emerald Lake is a must see for tourists visiting Yoho National Park. The natural beauty of British Columbia is the reason why everyone wants to visit this location so they can see the colorful water of Emerald Lake because they want to see emerald color in a lake that is so special in the world. Therefore, it will enhance a good experience when they see the color of Emerald Lake. In addition, Emerald Lake is contributing to a memory experience for those who visit. The lake's attractiveness is enhanced by its untouched natural surroundings.
